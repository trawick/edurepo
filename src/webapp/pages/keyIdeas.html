Background

  • Your student's 5th period math class, for example, follows a course curriculum.
  • The course curriculum consists primarily of specific learning objectives to be achieved in the course.
  • Teachers, students, and parents use many types of educational resources — text, videos, worksheets, games, etc. — in the context of a specific learning objective.

Existing problems to be addressed

  1. a large amount of duplicate effort on the part of teachers preparing or adjusting materials (even cutting and pasting) to consume in software ranging from Microsoft Word to Quizlet
  2. difficulties for parents to understand current objectives, often making it impractical for parents to follow the progress of classes and discuss class topics in a meaningful way
  3. ineffective systems created by states and private groups to index available materials, which do not allow direct access to materials which are relevant to current study

Key features of the project

  1. The current learning objectives for a class are easily communicated by the teacher to students and parents.
  2. The system provides a repository for various types of educational materials, all supporting targeted access by learning objective. A very important type of material is the course curriculum itself. Teachers will be able to use the repository to collaborate on and reuse materials without the burden of self publishing.
  3. Users of the system will be able to promote outside resources which apply to specific learning objectives.
  4. All materials in the system are freely available by end users and, more importantly, by end user software.

Expected outcome of the project

  1. Relating all materials to specific learning objectives will allow teachers, students, and parents to quickly find appropriate materials. For parents specifically, it will become much more easy to track exactly what their children are currently working on, and quickly find information to help them with their homework.
  2. Teachers will have a much easier means to communicate general class activity to parents.
  3. The open access of the system will support a rich ecosystem with incentives to educators of collaborate on materials and incentives to software developers to offer creative ways to use them.

In short

  • Software for smartphones, tablets, web sites, computers, game systems, and specialty devices will make relevant educational materials ubiquitous. Whether a child studied the Pythagorean theorem, the Napoleonic Wars, or the conjugation of common French verbs at school today, it should not take more than a few taps on her parent's smartphone or computer to access relevant materials and start a conversation about it.
  • Teachers who donate materials to the system will enable those materials to be consumed in many different ways by students all over the world.
